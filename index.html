뚜두뚜두 (Ddu-Du Ddu-Du)

The song is described by Billboard, as a "fierce hip-pop track overflowing with charisma "which "features a prominent trap beat". Forbes noted, the song has "combining crisp trap beats, breezy synth hooks, and infectious vocal melodies." Was written and produced by Teddy and co-produced by Bekuh Boom, 24 and R.Tee. 'Ddu-Du Ddu-Du' is the sound of gunfire, according to BlackPink. They are shooting guns of feminine charm into the hearts of their “lover” To understand it you need to understand their storyline.

Kill This Love

The song is described by YG Entertainment as being more "intense" than previous BLACKPINK songs but it has a "strong rhythm" like "Ddu-Du Ddu-Du", and that the choreography will be more "dynamic" than their previous choreography. 'Kill This Love' is about both lovers who ended up getting hurt by love, it's not beneficial, so they decide to break up before it does more damage.

붐바야 (BOOMBAYAH)

"Boombayah" was released on August 8, 2016 at 8 p.m. KST as a digital single titled Square One, coupled with "Whistle", through various digital music portals in South Korea. They are the playgirls, and throughout the song they get attracted to the guy(their lover) more and more.

"As If It's Your Last" is described as BLACKPINK's most exciting and upbeat song that they have released so far. BLACKPINK member Jisoo said that the group had thus far in their history only had "Black" concepts up until then and that this new single would be a "Pink" concept. The song was described as a "shimmery mix of synth-pop, house, reggae and moombathon music with disco grooves and change of sound from their previous releases.
